## Fermentation ![](img/yeast.jpg) > Preserving or modifying foods through microbial action Notes: * Examples: yoghurt/cheese, bread, wine/beer/cider, sauerkraut, vinegar, coffee, chocolate, salami * Microbes: bacteria, moulds, yeasts * Brewing = Fermentation for Alcohol * Yeasts digest sugars and starches into alcohol and CO2 * Bees use fermentation (pickled pollen) but protect against it with capped honey
## Ancient Mead ![](img/rock-art.jpg "") ![Chinese Fermentation Vessels](img/chinese-vessels-jiahu.jpg "Chinese Fermentation Vessels") Notes: * Many animals eat fermented (rotting) fruit. Ancient people did the same. * Honey hunters - probably from 40,000BCE (cave art in Spain, 6000BCE) * Earliest known fermented beverage: Jiahu, China, 6,000BCE, rice, honey, grape and hawthorn (Braggot?) * Chinese pottery dates back to 13,000BCE
